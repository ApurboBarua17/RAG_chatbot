=== FOUNDATIONAL COURSES ===

CSC 110 - Introduction to Computer Programming I (4 units)
Description: Introduction to programming concepts using Python. Topics include variables, control flow, functions, lists, and basic algorithms.
Prerequisites: C or higher in MATH 112 or appropriate math placement score.
Offered: Fall, Spring (sometimes Summer)

CSC 120 - Introduction to Computer Programming II (4 units)
Description: Continuation of CSC 110. Topics include object-oriented programming, recursion, data structures, and algorithm analysis.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 110 (or ECE 101 or ISTA 130).
Offered: Fall, Spring (sometimes Summer)

CSC 144 - Discrete Mathematics for Computer Science I (3 units)
Description: Logic, sets, functions, relations, induction, and combinatorics with applications to computer science.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 110 (or ECE 101 or ISTA 130) AND C or higher in MATH 112 or appropriate placement.
Substitutes: MATH 243 or MATH 323 may substitute.
Offered: Fall, Spring (sometimes Summer)

CSC 180 / CSC 196 - Introduction to Data Science Principles (3 units)
Description: Introduction to data science concepts, Python for data analysis, and basic statistical reasoning.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 110. Completion of Calculus I strongly recommended.
Note: Currently offered as CSC 196. Only offered in Spring semesters.

CSC 210 - Software Development (4 units)
Description: Software development methodology, data structures (stacks, queues, trees, graphs), algorithm design, and testing.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 120.
Offered: Fall, Spring (sometimes Summer)

CSC 244 - Discrete Mathematics for Computer Science II (3 units)
Description: Graph theory, trees, algorithms, formal languages, and finite automata.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 120 AND C or higher in CSC 144 (or MATH 243 or MATH 323).
Offered: Fall, Spring (sometimes Summer)

=== CORE UPPER-DIVISION COURSES (Advanced Standing Required) ===

CSC 252 - Computer Organization (3 units)
Description: Computer architecture, assembly language, memory hierarchy, pipelining, and digital logic.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 210 AND C or higher in CSC 244.
Equivalent: ECE 369A may substitute.

CSC 335 - Object-Oriented Programming and Design (3 units)
Description: Object-oriented design principles, design patterns, UML, testing, and software engineering best practices. Uses Java.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 210.

CSC 345 - Analysis of Discrete Structures (3 units)
Description: Algorithm analysis, sorting, searching, graph algorithms, dynamic programming, and complexity theory.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 244.
Note: Required for most upper-division CS electives.

CSC 352 - Systems Programming and Unix (3 units)
Description: C programming, Unix/Linux systems, processes, file I/O, memory management, and shell scripting.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 210.

CSC 380 - Principles of Data Science (3 units)
Description: Data wrangling, exploratory analysis, machine learning fundamentals, and data visualization using Python.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 210 AND C or higher in MATH 163 or MATH 263 (Statistics).
Equivalent: DATA 363 or MATH 363 may substitute.

=== PARADIGMS AREA ELECTIVES ===

CSC 343 - Human Computer Interaction (3 units)
Description: UI/UX design principles, usability testing, prototyping, and accessibility.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing.

CSC 372 - Comparative Programming Languages (3 units)
Description: Survey of programming language paradigms: functional, logic, scripting, and object-oriented languages.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 210.

CSC 422 - Introduction to Parallel and Distributed Programming (3 units)
Description: Parallel computing models, threads, distributed systems, and concurrent programming.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 352.

CSC 460 - Database Design (3 units)
Description: Relational databases, SQL, ER modeling, normalization, transactions, and NoSQL.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 345 or CSC 380.

=== THEORY AND WRITING AREA ELECTIVES ===

CSC 437 - Geometric Algorithms (3 units)
Description: Computational geometry, convex hulls, triangulation, and geometric data structures.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 345.

CSC 445 - Algorithms (3 units)
Description: Advanced algorithm design and analysis: greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, network flow, NP-completeness.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 345.

CSC 450 - Algorithms in Bioinformatics (3 units)
Description: Sequence alignment, phylogenetics, and computational biology algorithms.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 345.

CSC 473 - Automata, Grammars and Languages (3 units)
Description: Formal languages, finite automata, context-free grammars, Turing machines, and computability.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 345.

=== SYSTEMS AREA ELECTIVES ===

CSC 452 - Principles of Operating Systems (3 units)
Description: Process management, scheduling, memory management, file systems, and concurrency.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 252 AND C or higher in CSC 352.

CSC 453 - Compilers and Systems Software (3 units)
Description: Lexical analysis, parsing, semantic analysis, code generation, and optimization.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 345 AND C or higher in CSC 352.

=== AI / MACHINE LEARNING ELECTIVES ===

CSC 480 - Principles of Machine Learning (3 units)
Description: Supervised and unsupervised learning, neural networks, model evaluation, and ML pipelines.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 380 AND C or higher in CSC 345. MATH 313 (Linear Algebra) strongly recommended.

CSC 477 - Introduction to Computer Vision (3 units)
Description: Image processing, feature detection, deep learning for vision, and object recognition.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 380 AND MATH 313 (Linear Algebra).

CSC 438 - Computational Linguistics (3 units)
Description: Natural language processing, parsing, syntax, and semantics. Cross-listed with LING 438 and PSY 438.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing.

CSC 439 - Statistical Natural Language Processing (3 units)
Description: Statistical models for NLP, language models, machine translation, and text classification.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 380.
Cross-listed: DATA 439, ISTA 439, LING 439.

CSC 455 - Philosophy and Artificial Intelligence (3 units)
Description: Philosophical foundations of AI, ethics, consciousness, and the nature of intelligence.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing.
Cross-listed: PHIL 455.

CSC 483 - Text Retrieval and Web Search (3 units)
Description: Information retrieval, indexing, ranking algorithms, and search engine design.
Prerequisites: Advanced Standing. C or higher in CSC 380.

=== OTHER POPULAR ELECTIVES ===

CSC 317 - Mobile Application Programming (3 units)
Description: iOS or Android app development, mobile UI design, and device APIs.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 210.

CSC 337 - Web Programming (3 units)
Description: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, and full-stack web development.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 210.

CSC 346 - Cloud Computing (3 units)
Description: Cloud platforms (AWS/GCP/Azure), distributed systems, containers, and serverless computing.
Prerequisites: C or higher in CSC 210.

=== PREREQUISITE CHAIN SUMMARY ===

To reach CSC 345 (required for most upper-div electives):
CSC 110 → CSC 120 + CSC 144 → CSC 210 + CSC 244 → [Advanced Standing] → CSC 345

To reach CSC 452 (Operating Systems):
CSC 110 → CSC 120 → CSC 210 → CSC 252 AND CSC 352 → CSC 452

To reach CSC 480 (Machine Learning):
CSC 110 → CSC 120 → CSC 210 + MATH 163/263 → CSC 380 → CSC 480 (also needs CSC 345)

To reach CSC 453 (Compilers):
Need CSC 345 AND CSC 352 → CSC 453

Full CS foundational chain (3 semesters):
Semester 1: CSC 110
Semester 2: CSC 120, CSC 144, CSC 180/196
Semester 3: CSC 210, CSC 244

=== ADVANCED STANDING REQUIREMENTS (BS CS) ===

To progress from foundational to Advanced Standing, a student must have:
1. Completed all 6 foundational courses: CSC 110, CSC 120, CSC 144, CSC 180, CSC 210, CSC 244
2. Cumulative UA GPA of 2.4 or higher
3. GPA of 3.0 or higher in best attempts at CSC 120, CSC 210, and CSC 244 (taken at UA or elsewhere)
4. GPA of 2.0 or higher in all attempts at CSC courses taken at UA
5. At least two programming courses completed at UA from: CSC 110, 120, 210, 252, 317, 335, 337, 343, 346, 352, 372, 380

=== ADVANCED STANDING REQUIREMENTS (BS AI) ===

To progress to Advanced Standing in AI, a student must have:
1. Cumulative UA GPA of 2.4 or higher
2. GPA of 3.0 or higher in best attempts at CSC 120, CSC 210, CSC 244, and the AI intro course
3. GPA of 2.0 or higher in all attempts at CSC courses taken at UA
4. At least two programming courses completed at UA

=== BS CS CORE REQUIREMENTS (After Advanced Standing) ===

Required core (5 courses):
- CSC 252 - Computer Organization
- CSC 335 - Object-Oriented Programming and Design
- CSC 345 - Analysis of Discrete Structures
- CSC 352 - Systems Programming and Unix
- CSC 380 - Principles of Data Science (or DATA 363 / MATH 363)

Track electives required:
- 1 Paradigms elective: CSC 343, 372, 422, or 460
- 1 Theory/Writing elective: CSC 437, 445, 450, or 473
- 1 Systems elective: CSC 452 or CSC 453
- 3 additional electives (upper-division CSC courses)

Supporting math:
- Calculus I (MATH 122B or MATH 125)
- Calculus II or Linear Algebra (MATH 129 or MATH 313)
- Two science lab sequences (Physics, Chemistry, Biology, or Geology)

Graduation requirements:
- Minimum 120 total units
- Minimum 54 units in the major
- Minimum 2.0 GPA in major coursework
- Minimum 30 units of upper-division coursework
- Minimum 18 units of residency at UA

=== BS AI CORE REQUIREMENTS (After Advanced Standing) ===

Required core (3 courses):
- CSC 345 - Analysis of Discrete Structures
- CSC 380 - Principles of Data Science
- CSC 480 - Principles of Machine Learning

Required advanced AI electives (choose 4):
- CSC 438 - Computational Linguistics
- CSC 439 - Statistical Natural Language Processing
- CSC 455 - Philosophy and Artificial Intelligence
- CSC 477 - Introduction to Computer Vision
- CSC 483 - Text Retrieval and Web Search
- DATA/LING/ISTA 439 (cross-listed options)

Supporting math for AI:
- Calculus I (MATH 122B or MATH 125)
- Statistics (MATH 163 or MATH 263) — required before Advanced Standing
- Linear Algebra (MATH 313) — strongly recommended for CSC 477 and CSC 480

Graduation requirements:
- Minimum 120 total units
- Minimum 54 units in the major
- Minimum 2.0 GPA in major coursework
- Minimum 33 units of upper-division major coursework
- Minimum 18 units of residency at UA
